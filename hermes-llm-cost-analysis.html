3.2 How the Cache Multiplier Works

Cache economics are determined by three interacting variables: (1) cache-read price — what you pay per 1M cached tokens, (2) cache hit rate — what fraction of input tokens hit cache, (3) sticker input price — what you pay for the cache-miss fraction. The formula:

Effective Input $/M = (hit_rate × cache_read) + ((1 − hit_rate) × sticker_input)

At this user's volume, the hit_rate × cache_read term dominates because hit_rate is 91–96% for S-tier models. The sticker_input × (1−hit_rate) term is 4–9% of the total. This is why models like Step 3.7 Flash ($0.20 sticker, $0.04 cache, 77% hit) cost 3.6× more than DeepSeek V4 Pro ($0.435 sticker, $0.0036 cache, 96% hit) — despite having a cheaper sticker.

Critical insight: Cache-read price × 15.5B cache-hit tokens = your real bill. Sticker input price × 0.6B cache-miss tokens barely matters. A model with the cheapest sticker but no cache (Qwen3.5 Flash at $0.065/M) costs 2.8× more than DeepSeek with its $0.435 sticker — because 96% of DeepSeek's input costs $0.0036/M.
