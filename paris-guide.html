♿ Mobility Guide

One member of the group recently had a fall and is recovering — can walk but should avoid strain: long distances, steep hills, and lots of stairs. Every attraction below is rated for physical demand.

🟢 LOW Flat, seated, or elevator available. Minimal walking.
🟡 MOD Some walking or a few stairs. Benches usually available.
🟠 HIGH Significant walking, stairs, or hills. Pace yourself.
🔴 VERY HIGH Stairs only, no elevator, steep terrain. Skip or take very slow.
🦯 General Paris Accessibility Notes

Paris is old — many buildings predate elevators. Cobblestones in historic neighborhoods are uneven. Métro stations often have stairs-only access (only Line 14 is fully step-free). Buses are the best public transport for limited mobility — all have ramps and dedicated wheelchair spaces. Plan around buses + taxis/Uber rather than the Métro if walking is a concern.

💡 Strategy: Sight-See, Then Sit

Almost every attraction has a café nearby or inside. The Parisian way is to do one thing, then sit at a café terrace for 30–45 minutes before the next. This isn't laziness — it's how locals tour their own city. Build café stops between every landmark.

🏛️ Iconic Landmarks

🗼
Eiffel Tower
Best viewed from Champ de Mars (flat park with benches) or Trocadéro plaza. Go up at sunset. Elevator to all levels — no stairs required. Long queues for the lift; book ahead online to skip the worst of it.
🟡 MOD
Elevator available. Walking around base/Champ de Mars is 🟢 flat. Queuing for lift = standing ~30min — bring a cane seat if needed.
9:00–23:45 daily · Book ahead · €12–28
🏛️
Arc de Triomphe
284-step spiral staircase to the top. NO elevator for visitors. The view is incredible — but it's earned. Underground tunnel from Champs-Élysées (flat) to reach the base. Viewing from outside at ground level is still impressive and 🟢 no-stairs.
🔴 VERY HIGH
284 steps, no lift. Ground-level viewing is 🟢 — skip the climb for your friend.
10:00–22:30 · Open daily · €16
⛪
Notre-Dame
Fully reopened December 2024. Flat entry, smooth stone floors, seating in the nave. FREE with timed reservation online. The towers (387 steps, no lift) are a separate ticket — skip those.
🟢 LOW
Flat access, benches inside. Timed entry avoids standing in line. Towers are 🔴 stairs-only — skip.
Daily · Free · Reserve slot online · Towers: 🔴 387 steps, no lift
⛪
Sacré-Cœur
Montmartre's white basilica on the highest point in Paris. The funicular (cable car) goes up the hill — use it, don't walk the stairs. Regular Métro ticket works. Basilica interior is flat with seating. Dome climb is 300 steps — skip it.
🟡 MOD
Take the funicular up (Métro ticket, runs every 6min). Basilica = 🟢 flat. Skip the dome (300 steps 🔴).
6:00–22:30 · Free basilica · Funicular: standard Métro ticket
🪟
Sainte-Chapelle
15 stained-glass windows, 1,113 biblical scenes. Upper chapel requires climbing a narrow spiral staircase — no elevator. The lower chapel (flat entry) is also beautiful but the glass is upstairs. Worth it if your friend can manage ~40 steps slowly with a handrail.
🟠 HIGH
Narrow spiral stairs to upper chapel (~40 steps), no lift. Lower chapel is 🟢 flat but the glass is upstairs.
9:00–17:00 · Open daily · €13
🏛️
Panthéon
Foucault's pendulum, vast nave, crypt of France's greats. Main floor is flat and spacious. Crypt is down stairs (handrail, ~50 steps). Dome is 🔴 206 steps — skip entirely.
🟡 MOD
Main floor 🟢 flat. Crypt: ~50 steps down with handrail. Dome: 🔴 206 steps — skip.
10:00–18:30 · €13
🏰
Hôtel des Invalides
Napoleon's tomb under a golden dome. The Army Museum on-site is world-class. Elevator available. Flat courtyard, wide galleries. One of the most accessible major attractions in Paris. Napoleon's tomb viewing is wheelchair-friendly.
🟢 LOW
Elevator to all levels. Wide, flat galleries. Excellent accessibility overall.
10:00–18:00 daily · €15 · Closed 1st Monday monthly
🎭
Palais Garnier (Opéra)
The Phantom of the Opera's real home. Chagall ceiling, Grand Foyer, marble staircase. Elevator available for most areas. Self-guided or guided tours. The auditorium itself may have steps depending on route — ask at the entrance for the step-free path.
🟢 LOW
Elevator access to most areas. Ask for the accessible route at entry. Some upper balcony areas may require stairs.
10:00–17:00 · €15 · Closed days with matinees
🚢
Seine River Cruise
The ultimate low-mobility activity. See the Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame, Louvre, Musée d'Orsay, and 10+ bridges — all while sitting down. 1-hour loop. Most boats have ramps and accessible boarding. Go at sunset.
🟢 LOW
Sit the whole time. Most companies have ramp boarding. Vedettes du Pont Neuf and Bateaux Mouches are wheelchair-friendly.
~€15–20 · Hourly departures · No booking needed for daytime

🎨 Museums — Pick Your Vibe

MuseumWhy GoMobilityDetails
Louvre Mona Lisa, Winged Victory, Venus de Milo. 35,000 pieces across 73,000 m². Warning: enormous. Walking the full museum is 12+ km. 🟠 HIGH Elevators available. Wheelchairs loaned free. Pick ONE wing — don't try to see it all. The distance between galleries is the real strain. Go Wed/Fri evening (less crowded). Closed Tuesdays.
Musée d'Orsay World's best Impressionist collection in a converted train station. Van Gogh, Monet, Renoir, Degas. More manageable than the Louvre. 🟡 MOD Elevators + escalators. Flat main hall. Benches throughout. Much better for limited mobility than the Louvre. Closed Mondays.
Musée de l'Orangerie Monet's Water Lilies in two oval rooms built to his specs. Meditative. Small, intimate — 1 hour is enough. 🟢 LOW Single floor, flat, benches in the Water Lilies rooms. Elevator available. Best museum pick for limited mobility. Closed Tuesdays.
Musée Rodin The Thinker, The Gates of Hell, gorgeous sculpture garden. Indoor galleries + outdoor garden. 🟡 MOD Indoor: elevator, flat. Garden: gravel paths, flat but uneven. Benches throughout the garden. Closed Mondays.
Musée Carnavalet History of Paris — from Roman Lutetia to Revolution to today. FREE permanent collection. Gorgeous 16th-century mansion setting. 🟡 MOD Elevator available. Some narrow historic doorways. Free, so you can leave whenever without guilt. Closed Mondays.
Musée Picasso 5,000+ Picasso works in a beautiful Marais mansion. His personal collection — paintings, sculptures, sketches. 🟡 MOD Elevator available in parts. Some stairs between levels. Intimate scale — 1.5 hours covers it. Closed Mondays.
Musée Marmottan Monet The largest Monet collection in the world — including Impression, Sunrise, the painting that named Impressionism. Quiet, off the tourist track. 🟢 LOW Small, mainly single floor. Elevator available. Far fewer crowds than d'Orsay. Closed Mondays.
Centre Pompidou Modern art, inside-out building, best rooftop views 🔴 SKIP CLOSED until 2030 — full renovation. Not a mobility issue, just not an option.
🎟️ Museum Pass Strategy

The Paris Museum Pass (2-day: €55, 4-day: €70) covers most of these. With limited mobility, the 4-day pass is better — do one museum per morning, café lunch, one more sight, then rest. Don't cram 3 museums in a day.

🏘️ Neighborhoods to Wander

🏳️‍🌈
Le Marais
Trendy, historic, LGBTQ+ hub. Best falafel in Paris (L'As du Fallafel), boutique shopping, Place des Vosges (flat park with benches under arcades). Mostly flat — pre-Haussmann streets are narrow but level.
🟢 LOW
Flat terrain, many cafés for rest stops. Place des Vosges has shaded benches.
3rd & 4th arr.
🎨
Montmartre
Village-on-a-hill feel. Skip touristy Place du Tertre — go behind Sacré-Cœur to rue de l'Abreuvoir and La Maison Rose. Steep cobblestone streets.
🟠 HIGH
Very hilly, cobblestones, few benches on slopes. Take funicular to Sacré-Cœur, explore the flat summit area, taxi back down.
18th arr. · Funicular from Anvers Métro
🧑‍🎨
Canal Saint-Martin
Grab wine, cheese, baguette from a nearby shop. Sit by the canal at golden hour. Pure Paris. Flat towpaths, iron footbridges. Benches along the water.
🟢 LOW
Completely flat, benches everywhere. Park near République or Jacques Bonsergent Métro for shortest walk to the water.
10th arr. · Perfect for a picnic
📚
Saint-Germain-des-Prés
Literary Paris. Café de Flore, Les Deux Magots, bookshops, jazz. Flat boulevards, wide sidewalks. The café terraces ARE the attraction — sit and watch.
🟢 LOW
Flat, wide sidewalks, cafés every 10 meters. The ideal neighborhood for café-hopping with limited walking.
6th arr.
📖
Latin Quarter
Shakespeare & Company bookshop, Sorbonne, narrow medieval streets, cheap student eats. Some streets slope toward the Seine — mild inclines, not Montmartre-steep.
🟡 MOD
Mild slopes, some cobblestones. Plenty of cafés and bookshop seating. Stick to rue Saint-Jacques (flat) and the riverbank.
5th arr.
🍸
South Pigalle (SoPi)
Nightlife central. Cocktail bars, natural wine, where Parisians go out. Rue des Martyrs is the artery. Some incline but manageable.
🟡 MOD
Gentle slope up rue des Martyrs. Bars have seating. Go by taxi in the evening to save steps.
9th arr. · After dark
🏘️
Île Saint-Louis
The smaller of Paris's two islands. Berthillon ice cream, 17th-century architecture, no major sights — just beautiful streets. Flat, quiet, one main street with benches.
🟢 LOW
Flat, short distances, benches along the quays. Combine with Notre-Dame (next door on Île de la Cité).
4th arr. · Walk from Notre-Dame

🌳 Parks & Gardens — Rest Your Legs

🌳
Jardin du Luxembourg
The most beautiful park in Paris. Medici Fountain, orchard, tennis courts, iconic green chairs. Flat gravel paths, benches everywhere, shaded. The green chairs are free — grab one and park yourself for an hour.
🟢 LOW
Flat, chairs everywhere, café inside the park. Enter from the Odéon side for the flattest approach.
6th arr. · Free · Opens 7:30am
🌷
Jardin des Tuileries
Between the Louvre and Place de la Concorde. Formal French gardens, fountains, sculptures. Flat, wide gravel paths, green chairs around the fountains. Perfect rest stop between Louvre and Orangerie.
🟢 LOW
Flat, chairs, cafés. Directly between Louvre and Orangerie — ideal mid-day rest.
1st arr. · Free · Always open
🌸
Jardin des Plantes
Botanical garden with a zoo, natural history museum, rose garden, and alpine garden. Flat paths, benches, less crowded than Luxembourg. The rose garden in June is spectacular.
🟢 LOW
Flat, many benches, café. Combine with the Grande Mosquée de Paris next door for mint tea in their courtyard.
5th arr. · Free gardens · Museum has entry fee
🏞️
Parc Monceau
English-style garden in the 8th. Roman columns, picturesque bridge, duck pond. Flat, winding paths, benches, carousel. A local secret — almost zero tourists.
🟢 LOW
Flat, benches, very compact — you can see it all in 30 minutes of gentle walking.
8th arr. · Free · Opens 7:00am
🏛️
Place des Vosges
Paris's oldest planned square (1605). Perfect red-brick symmetry, arcades, fountain, grass. Sit under the arcades — shaded benches — or on the lawn. Victor Hugo's house is here (free museum).
🟢 LOW
Flat, arcade benches shaded, cafés on the square. Combine with a Marais walk.
4th arr. · Free · Always open

🔍 Hidden Gems

🛒
Marché des Enfants Rouges
Oldest covered market in Paris, est. 1615. Moroccan, Japanese, French stalls at communal tables. Eat anything — it's all good.
🟢 LOW
Flat, seating available, compact. Perfect lunch stop in the Marais.
3rd arr. · Tue–Sat until 8:30pm, Sun until 5pm
🏛️
Covered Passages
19th-century glass-roofed shopping arcades. Galerie Vivienne, Passage des Panoramas, Passage Jouffroy. Time capsules with bookshops and cafés. Flat, sheltered from rain, seating in cafés.
🟢 LOW
Fully flat, indoors-ish, cafés with seating. Start at Galerie Vivienne (most beautiful).
2nd & 9th arr.
🌹
Musée de la Vie Romantique
Free, tiny, charming museum in a former artist's home. Tearoom garden at the back — perfect quiet escape. Flat entry, garden seating.
🟢 LOW
Flat, garden with seating, small scale. The tearoom is the real draw.
9th arr. · Free entry · Closed Mondays
🪦
Père Lachaise Cemetery
Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Chopin, Edith Piaf. 44 hectares of tree-lined paths. Hilly, cobblestone paths, very spread out. Get a map and target specific graves — don't wander aimlessly.
🟠 HIGH
Hilly, cobblestones, huge area. Enter at Gambetta (top) and walk downhill. Target 2–3 graves, not the whole cemetery.
20th arr. · Free · Maps at entrance
☕
Grande Mosquée de Paris
Beautiful Hispano-Moorish architecture from 1926. Courtyard café serving mint tea and North African pastries. Tiled courtyard with fountains — an oasis of calm. Flat, seated.
🟢 LOW
Flat courtyard, seated café. Combine with Jardin des Plantes next door.
5th arr. · Café open Tue–Sun 9:00–18:00
🌆
Belleville Park
Best free skyline view. Zero tourists. Bring a beer from the corner store. On a hill. The park itself has benches at the viewpoint, but getting there is uphill.
🟠 HIGH
Uphill to reach. Take a taxi to the park entrance, then it's a short flat walk to the viewpoint benches. Don't walk up from the Métro.
20th arr. · Free · Open until dusk
🏠
La Maison Rose
That pink corner house on rue de l'Abreuvoir in Montmartre. Instagram-famous for good reason — especially in golden hour.
🟠 HIGH
Requires walking Montmartre's hilly streets to reach. Taxi as close as possible, or view from the Petit Train de Montmartre (tourist train, seated).
18th arr. · Montmartre

🍽️ Where Locals Actually Eat — Bistros

Classic French, affordable, where Parisians go for steak frites and a glass of red. All have seating — no standing required.

PlaceArr.VibeWhat to Order
Bouillon Pigalle 18th 300-seat brasserie, always buzzing. True bouillon — quality food at mass prices. Flat entry, spacious. Can reserve online to skip the line (which can be long). Egg mayo (€3!), steak frites, onion soup
Les Arlots 10th SoPi gem. Daily changing menu, hearty comfort food, under €30. Small — reserve ahead. Flat street-level entry. Whatever's on the board — trust the kitchen
Le Bon Georges 9th Wine List of the Year 2024. Corner bistro that transforms into a wine-lover's evening spot. Flat entry. Chef's tartare, chocolate mousse, côte de boeuf for two
Bouillon Chartier 9th Historic 1896 dining hall. Waiters in waistcoats write your order on the paper tablecloth. Flat entry from the street. Can be noisy. Escargots, confit de canard, crème brûlée
Bouillon République 11th Sister to Pigalle, same concept, newer. Larger space, easier to get a table. Flat entry, accessible restroom. Less chaotic than Pigalle. Same bouillon classics — egg mayo, steak frites
Chez Janou 3rd Provençal bistro near Place des Vosges. Chocolate mousse served from a giant bowl — they scoop as much as you want. Flat entry, tight interior — request a ground-floor table when booking. Chocolate mousse (famous), ratatouille, pastis

🥐 Must-Try Foods

  • 🥐 Croissant au beurre — From any proper boulangerie (not a chain). Look for "Artisan Boulanger" on the window. A real croissant shatters when you bite it.
  • 🥖 Baguette tradition — The crust should crackle audibly when you squeeze it. A good baguette needs no butter.
  • 🥩 Steak frites — Avec sauce au poivre (peppercorn sauce), at any proper bistro with a glass of Côtes du Rhône.
  • 🧅 Soupe à l'oignon — French onion soup under bubbling melted gruyère. Winter essential, summer acceptable.
  • 🐌 Escargots — Six or twelve, swimming in garlic-parsley butter. Soak up the leftover butter with bread. Just do it.
  • 🍮 Crème brûlée — The crack of the caramelized top is the sound of vacation. Vanilla, not flavored — keep it classic.
  • 🧀 Cheese board — Go to a fromagerie, ask "qu'est-ce que vous recommandez aujourd'hui?" Let them build you a plate. Pair with a Sancerre.
  • 🌈 Macarons — Pierre Hermé > Ladurée. Try the Ispahan (rose, lychee, raspberry) at Hermé. The Mogador (milk chocolate, passion fruit) is also transcendent.
  • 🦆 Confit de canard — Duck leg slow-cooked in its own fat until the meat falls off the bone. Crispy skin, tender flesh. Order it anywhere it's on the menu.
  • 🥞 Galette complète — Buckwheat crêpe with ham, gruyère, and a fried egg. From Brittany, perfected in Paris. Best eaten with dry Breton cider.
🥐 Boulangerie vs. Pâtisserie

Boulangerie = bread, croissants, savory. Pâtisserie = pastries, cakes, tarts, macarons. Many are both, but the best specialize. Get your morning croissant at a boulangerie, your afternoon éclair at a pâtisserie.

🍷 Local Tips — Don't Look Like a Tourist

☕ Coffee

Stand at the bar (comptoir) — it's half the price of sitting. But with mobility concerns: sit. Pay the extra €2. Your friend needs the rest. An espresso at a table is €3–4 and you get to stay as long as you want — that's cheap rent for a Parisian terrace.

💧 Water

Tap water is free and excellent. Ask for "une carafe d'eau". Restaurants are legally required to provide it. Don't pay €6 for bottled.

💰 Tipping

Service is included (15% service compris). Tips not expected. Round up or leave €1–2 for great service.

🕖 Dinner Time

Restaurants open at 7:00–7:30 PM. Eating at 6pm marks you as a tourist (kitchens won't be ready). Peak dinner: 8:30–9:30pm. Book ahead for any bistro on this list — they fill up.

🥖 Bread Rules

Bread is refilled free. It goes on the table, not a bread plate. Never bite the baguette on the way home — you'll eat the whole thing.

🚌 Getting Around with Limited Mobility

Use buses, not the Métro. Paris buses are excellent — all have ramps, designated seats, and you see the city while you ride. Bus lines 69, 72, and 87 pass most major sights. Uber/Bolt are plentiful and reasonably priced. Only Métro Line 14 is fully step-free — the other 15 lines all have significant stairs.

👋 Greetings Matter

Always say "Bonjour" entering a shop or restaurant, "Merci, au revoir" leaving. Skipping this is genuinely rude. It opens doors — literally and figuratively.

🦯 Public Toilets

Paris has free public sanisettes (self-cleaning pods) throughout the city — flat entry, accessible. Every café also has a toilet for customers (buy an espresso = toilet access). Museums all have accessible restrooms. Plan toilet stops — don't assume you'll find one on a random street corner.

Arrondissement Cheat Sheet

Paris spirals — 1st is center, 20th is outer. Lower numbers = more central. Stay in 1st–7th for flat terrain and short distances. The 10th/11th/18th offer value but more hills. Always confirm elevator and step-free entry — many charming Parisian hotels are in historic buildings without lifts.

📱 Book Hotels on Booking.com with "Elevator" and "Ground Floor" Filters

Paris hotels are legally required to disclose accessibility features. Filter for elevator + ground-floor rooms available. Message the hotel before booking to confirm — "Mon ami a des difficultés à marcher. Avez-vous une chambre accessible sans escaliers?"

📋 Quick Low-Mobility Checklist

Best: Seine cruise, Notre-Dame, Invalides, Opéra Garnier, Orangerie, Luxembourg/Tuileries gardens, covered passages, Marché des Enfants Rouges
Good with care: Sacré-Cœur (funicular), Louvre (one wing), Musée d'Orsay, Rodin garden, Panthéon (main floor), Canal Saint-Martin
