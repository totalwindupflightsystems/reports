🏛️ Iconic Landmarks

🗼
Eiffel Tower
Best viewed from Champ de Mars or Trocadéro plaza. Go up at sunset for the golden hour cityscape.
9:00–23:45 daily · Book ahead
🏛️
Arc de Triomphe
Climb 284 steps to the top for the best Champs-Élysées panorama. Better than the Eiffel Tower view — you're IN the skyline.
10:00–22:30 · Open daily
⛪
Notre-Dame
Fully reopened December 2024 after the fire restoration. Stunning inside. FREE entry with timed slot online.
Daily · Reserve slot online
⛪
Sacré-Cœur
Montmartre's white basilica. Go at sunrise before the crowds. Dome climb for the second-best view in Paris.
6:00–22:30 · Free basilica
🪟
Sainte-Chapelle
15 massive stained-glass windows, 1,113 biblical scenes. Go on a sunny day — the light through the glass will ruin all other churches for you.
9:00–17:00 · Open daily
🏛️
Panthéon
Foucault's pendulum swinging in the nave. Crypt holds France's greats: Voltaire, Rousseau, Hugo, Curie.
10:00–18:30 · Closed some holidays

🔍 Hidden Gems

🛒
Marché des Enfants Rouges
Oldest covered market in Paris, est. 1615. Moroccan, Japanese, French stalls at communal tables. Eat anything — it's all good.
3rd arr. · Tue–Sat until 8:30pm, Sun until 5pm
🏛️
Covered Passages
19th-century glass-roofed shopping arcades. Galerie Vivienne, Passage des Panoramas, Passage Jouffroy. Time capsules with bookshops and cafés.
2nd & 9th arr.
🌹
Musée de la Vie Romantique
Free, tiny, charming museum in a former artist's home. Tearoom garden at the back — perfect quiet escape.
9th arr. · Free entry
🪦
Père Lachaise Cemetery
Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Chopin, Edith Piaf. Weirdly peaceful. 44 hectares of overgrown tombs and tree-lined paths.
20th arr. · Free entry
🌆
Belleville Park
Best free skyline view in Paris. Zero tourists. Bring a beer from the corner store and watch the sunset over the Eiffel Tower.
20th arr. · Open until dusk
🏠
La Maison Rose
That pink corner house you've seen on Instagram, on rue de l'Abreuvoir in Montmartre. Actually worth the photo — especially in golden hour.
18th arr. · Montmartre

🍽️ Where Locals Actually Eat — Bistros

Classic French, affordable, where Parisians go for steak frites and a glass of red.

PlaceArr.VibeWhat to Order
Bouillon Pigalle 18th 300-seat brasserie, always buzzing, near Montmartre. True bouillon — quality food at mass prices. Egg mayo (€3!), steak frites, onion soup
Les Arlots 10th SoPi gem. Daily changing menu, hearty comfort food, under €30. Always full of locals. Whatever's on the board — trust the kitchen
Le Bon Georges 9th Wine List of the Year 2024. Corner bistro that transforms into a wine-lover's evening spot. Chef's tartare, chocolate mousse, côte de boeuf for two
Bouillon Chartier 9th Historic 1896 dining hall. Waiters in waistcoats write your order on the paper tablecloth. An institution. Escargots, confit de canard, crème brûlée

🥐 Must-Try Foods

  • 🥐 Croissant au beurre — From any proper boulangerie (not a chain). Look for the "Artisan Boulanger" label on the window.
  • 🥖 Baguette tradition — The crust should crackle audibly when you squeeze it. A good baguette needs no butter.
  • 🥩 Steak frites — Avec sauce au poivre (peppercorn sauce), at any proper bistro with a glass of Côtes du Rhône.
  • 🧅 Soupe à l'oignon — French onion soup under a bubbling crust of melted gruyère. Winter essential, summer acceptable.
  • 🐌 Escargots — Just do it. Six or twelve, swimming in garlic-parsley butter. Soak up the leftover butter with bread.
  • 🍮 Crème brûlée — The crack of the caramelized top is the sound of vacation. Vanilla, not flavored — keep it classic.
  • 🧀 Cheese board — Go to a fromagerie, ask "qu'est-ce que vous recommandez aujourd'hui?" Let them build you a plate.
  • 🌈 Macarons — Pierre Hermé > Ladurée. Fight me. Try the Ispahan (rose, lychee, raspberry) at Hermé.
🥐 Boulangerie vs. Pâtisserie

Boulangerie = bread, croissants, savory. Pâtisserie = pastries, cakes, tarts, macarons. Many are both, but the best specialize. Get your morning croissant at a boulangerie, your afternoon éclair at a pâtisserie.

🍷 Local Tips — Don't Look Like a Tourist

☕ Coffee

Stand at the bar (comptoir) — it's half the price of sitting at a table. An espresso at the bar is €1.20–1.50. Same coffee at a table: €3–4.

💧 Water

Tap water is free and excellent in Paris. Ask for "une carafe d'eau" — don't pay €6 for bottled. Restaurants are legally required to provide free tap water.

💰 Tipping

Service is included in the price (15% service compris). Tips are not expected. Round up to the nearest euro or leave €1–2 for exceptional service. Americans: resist the urge to tip 20% — it marks you instantly.

🕖 Dinner Time

Restaurants open at 7:00–7:30 PM for dinner. Eating at 6pm marks you as a tourist (and many kitchens won't even be ready). Peak dinner hour is 8:30–9:30pm.

🥖 Bread Rules

Bread is refilled free at meals. It goes directly on the table (not a bread plate). Never bite the baguette on the way home — you'll eat the whole thing before you get there. This is a real risk.

🚇 Getting Around

The Métro is excellent — trains every 3–5 minutes. Buy a carnet of 10 tickets for a discount. But walk as much as possible — Paris is a walking city and you'll discover twice as much on foot.

👋 Greetings Matter

Always say "Bonjour" when entering a shop, restaurant, or even an elevator. Then "Merci, au revoir" when leaving. Skipping this is considered genuinely rude, not just touristy. It opens doors — literally and figuratively.

Arrondissement Cheat Sheet

Paris is arranged as a snail shell — the 1st is the center, spiraling out to the 20th. Lower numbers = more central, more expensive. Stay in the 1st–11th for walkability. The 10th, 11th, and 18th offer the best value-to-vibe ratio.
