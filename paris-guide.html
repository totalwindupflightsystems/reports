♿ Mobility Guide

One member of the group recently had a fall and is recovering — can walk but should avoid strain: long distances, steep hills, lots of stairs. Every attraction is rated for physical demand.

🟢 LOW Flat, seated, or elevator. Minimal walking.
🟡 MOD Some walking or a few stairs. Benches available.
🟠 HIGH Significant walking, stairs, or hills.
🔴 VERY HIGH Stairs only, no elevator. Skip or take very slow.
🦯 Paris Accessibility Reality

Paris is old — many buildings predate elevators. Cobblestones in historic neighborhoods are uneven. Métro stations often stairs-only (only Line 14 is fully step-free). Buses are best for limited mobility — all have ramps. Plan around buses + taxis/Uber, not the Métro.

💡 Strategy: Sight-See, Then Sit

Almost every attraction has a café nearby or inside. The Parisian way: do one thing, then sit at a café terrace for 30–45 minutes. This isn't laziness — it's how locals tour their own city. Build café stops between every landmark.

👑 Day Trip — Palace of Versailles & Marie Antoinette's Estate

The ultimate Paris day trip. Book a full day — you need it. 35 minutes by RER C train from central Paris.

🏰
Palace of Versailles
The most opulent palace in Europe. Hall of Mirrors, King's State Apartments, 2,300 rooms. Go Tuesday–Sunday (closed Mondays).
🟠 HIGH
Enormous — hours of walking. Wheelchairs available. Skip the crowds: book the 9am first slot.
👆 Click for prices & booking
🌸
Petit Trianon & Marie Antoinette's Hamlet
Marie Antoinette's private escape from court life. The Hamlet is a faux-rustic village she built to play shepherdess. The most romantic part of Versailles.
🟡 MOD
Gravel paths, some walking between buildings. Benches in the Hamlet gardens. The Petit Train shuttle connects the palace to Trianon — USE IT to save steps.
👆 Click for prices & booking
🌿
Versailles Gardens
800 hectares of formal French gardens. Musical Fountains Show (weekends spring–summer). Rent a golf cart or take the Petit Train — the gardens are vast.
🟡 MOD
Massive — use a golf cart (€38/hr) or the Petit Train. Fountains show is magical. Weekends only for fountains.
👆 Click for details
📋 Versailles Mobility Strategy

Rent a golf cart for the gardens — it's the single best thing you can do. Take the Petit Train from the palace to Petit Trianon (saves 2km of walking each way). Book the Passport with Timed Entry (€32) — covers everything. Go on a Tuesday or Wednesday (fewer crowds than weekends, and the fountains show runs Tuesdays too in summer).

💍 For Special Occasions

Proposal territory: L'Oiseau Blanc (2★, plane replica, €325 tasting), Girafe (terrace at golden hour), Les Ombres (glass roof, Eiffel sparkles at night). Book 3–4 weeks ahead for terrace tables at any of these. Mention it's a special occasion when booking — they'll often do something extra.

💡 Budget Strategy: Splurge on One, Save on the Rest

Pick ONE splurge dinner (€100–200/person) for the big night. Do rooftop cocktails (€18–25/drink) for sunset another night — same Eiffel Tower sparkle, fraction of the cost. Cafés like Angelina and Carette give you the Belle Époque glow for €15–20.

🥐 Must-Try Foods

  • 🥐Croissant au beurre — From any proper boulangerie (not a chain). Look for "Artisan Boulanger" on the window.
  • 🥖Baguette tradition — The crust should crackle audibly when you squeeze it.
  • 🥩Steak frites — Avec sauce au poivre, at any proper bistro with Côtes du Rhône.
  • 🧅Soupe à l'oignon — Under bubbling melted gruyère. Winter essential, summer acceptable.
  • 🐌Escargots — Swimming in garlic-parsley butter. Just do it.
  • 🍮Crème brûlée — Vanilla, not flavored. Keep it classic.
  • 🧀Cheese board — Ask the fromager: "qu'est-ce que vous recommandez?"
  • 🌈Macarons — Pierre Hermé > Ladurée. Try Ispahan (rose, lychee, raspberry).
  • 🦆Confit de canard — Duck leg slow-cooked in its own fat. Crispy skin, tender flesh.
  • 🥞Galette complète — Buckwheat crêpe with ham, gruyère, fried egg. Pair with dry cider.

🍷 Local Tips

☕ Coffee

Stand at bar = half price. But with mobility concerns: sit, pay the extra €2, rest. A terrace seat is cheap rent for a Parisian afternoon.

💧 Water

Tap water is free and excellent. Ask for "une carafe d'eau" — restaurants must provide it.

💰 Tipping

Service is included (15%). Tips not expected. Round up or leave €1–2 for great service.

🕖 Dinner Time

Restaurants open 7:00–7:30 PM. Peak: 8:30–9:30pm. Book ahead for any bistro on this list.

🚌 Getting Around

Use buses, not Métro. All Paris buses have ramps. Only Métro Line 14 is fully step-free. Bus lines 69, 72, 87 pass most sights. Uber/Bolt are plentiful.

👋 Greetings

Always "Bonjour" entering, "Merci, au revoir" leaving. Skipping this is genuinely rude.

🦯 Public Toilets

Free sanisettes (self-cleaning pods) throughout the city. Museums all have accessible restrooms. Every café toilet is yours for the price of an espresso.
