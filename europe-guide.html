🇨🇭 Switzerland — Panoramic Trains Through the Alps

⭐ Star's #1 priority. "The panoramic train is a whole experience." Switzerland's scenic rail journeys cut through the Alps past glaciers, viaducts, and mountain villages. This isn't just transportation — it's the main event. Wide panoramic windows, first-class dining cars, and views that will ruin all other train rides forever.

🚂 The Panoramic Trains

Glacier Express
The crown jewel. Zermatt → St. Moritz. 8 hours, 291 bridges, 91 tunnels. Panoramic windows, 3-course lunch served at your seat.
👆 Click for details
Bernina Express
Chur → Tirano (Italy). 4 hours, 196 bridges, 55 tunnels. UNESCO World Heritage route. Crosses the Alps from glaciers to palm trees.
👆 Click for details
🏔️
GoldenPass Express
Montreux → Interlaken. Lakes, vineyards, mountain passes. The newest panoramic train (2022). Prestige Class = rotating seats.
👆 Click for details
💡 Star's Dream: Excellence Class on the Glacier Express

The Excellence Class has 20 seats, a personal concierge, 5-course meal with wine pairing, and a guaranteed window seat with the best views. €420 per person. Book 3–6 months ahead — it sells out. This is the splurge that's worth every franc. Standard 1st class (€270) also has panoramic windows and a 3-course meal — still incredible.

🇮🇹 Rome — The Eternal City

Ancient ruins, Vatican grandeur, and pasta that will ruin Italian food everywhere else. Rome is loud, chaotic, and absolutely intoxicating. Give it at least 3 days — anything less and you'll feel robbed.

🌟 Top Sights

Colosseum
The iconic Roman amphitheater. Book the underground + arena floor tour.
👆 Click for details
Vatican Museums & St. Peter's
Sistine Chapel ceiling. Book early morning before public hours.
👆 Click for details
Trevi Fountain & Pantheon
Toss a coin. Then walk 5 min to the Pantheon — free, 2000 years old, still standing.
👆 Click for details

🇮🇹 Venice — The Floating City

No roads, just canals. It's exactly as magical as you've heard — and as crowded. Go early morning or late evening to have it to yourself. Get lost on purpose — every dead-end reveals something beautiful.

🌟 Top Sights

St. Mark's Square & Basilica
Golden mosaics, soaring domes. Doge's Palace next door. Book skip-the-line.
👆 Click for details
Rialto Bridge & Grand Canal
The iconic white marble bridge. Take a vaporetto (water bus) down the Grand Canal at sunset.
👆 Click for details
Murano & Burano Islands
Murano = glassblowing. Burano = rainbow-colored houses. The most photogenic spot in Italy.
👆 Click for details

🍷 Where to Eat

🐟
Alle Testiere
Tiny 22-seat seafood spot. Market-fresh daily menu. The best meal in Venice. Book well ahead.
👆 Click for details

🇮🇹 Florence — The Cradle of the Renaissance

Michelangelo's David, Brunelleschi's dome, the Uffizi. Florence is an open-air museum — but also home to the best steak in Italy. Compact and walkable; 2–3 days is enough.

🌟 Top Sights

Duomo & Brunelleschi's Dome
463 steps to the top. The view of Florence's terracotta roofs is worth every one.

🎨

👆 Click for details
🌉
Ponte Vecchio

👆 Click for details

🥩 Where to Eat

🇮🇹 Milan — Fashion, Design & the Duomo

Italy's capital of fashion and design. The Duomo is one of the world's most spectacular Gothic cathedrals — 3,400 statues, 135 spires. And the fashion museums Star mentioned? "They just look like a dream itself."

🌟 Top Sights

Duomo di Milano
The 5th-largest cathedral in the world. Walk on the rooftop among the spires — one of Europe's great experiences.
👆 Click for details
👗
Armani/Silos
⭐ Star's pick. Giorgio Armani's fashion museum — 4 floors of haute couture in a converted 1950s grain warehouse. "A dream itself."
👆 Click for details
🖼️
The Last Supper
Da Vinci's masterpiece at Santa Maria delle Grazie. 15-minute timed slots. Book 2–3 months ahead.
👆 Click for details
💡 Star's Fashion Museum Day

Armani/Silos (€12, book ahead) in the Tortona design district. Then for Dior: there's no standalone Dior museum in Milan, but the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II (the world's oldest shopping mall, stunning glass dome) has Dior, Prada, Gucci, and Louis Vuitton flagship stores. Also check current exhibitions at Palazzo Reale — it frequently hosts major fashion retrospectives.

🇮🇹 Amalfi Coast — "So Beautiful"

⭐ Star's words: "So beautiful." Pastel villages clinging to cliffs above the Tyrrhenian Sea. Lemon groves, bougainvillea, and winding coastal roads that make every turn a postcard. Positano is the icon, Ravello is the hidden gem, and the drive between them is the main event.

🌟 Top Towns

Positano
The postcard town. Pastel houses cascading down the cliff to a pebble beach. Boutique shopping, lemon granita, and the most photographed coastline in Italy.
👆 Click for details
Ravello
Perched high above the sea. Villa Rufolo and Villa Cimbrone gardens — the Terrace of Infinity is one of the most romantic views on Earth.
👆 Click for details
⛪
Amalfi Town
The coast's namesake. St. Andrew's Cathedral (Duomo di Amalfi), lemon sorbet served in a hollowed-out lemon, boat tours to hidden coves.
👆 Click for details
🚗 Getting Around

No train line on the coast — you'll drive (white-knuckle cliff roads), take the SITA bus (crowded but works), or hire a private driver (€€€ but worth it for one day). Base yourself in Sorrento (connected to Naples by train) and day-trip into the coast. Or stay in Positano if budget allows — waking up to that view is the point.

🇪🇸 Madrid — Art, Tapas & Late Nights

Spain's capital runs on art museums by day and tapas bars by night. Dinner at 10pm is normal. The Prado, Reina Sofía, and Thyssen form the "Golden Triangle of Art."

🌟 Top Sights

🖼️
Prado Museum
Velázquez, Goya, El Greco. One of the world's greatest collections. Free entry 6–8pm Mon–Sat.
👆 Click for details
👑
Royal Palace
The largest functioning royal palace in Europe. 3,418 rooms. Opulent beyond belief.
👆 Click for details
🌳
El Retiro Park
Madrid's green lung. Row a boat on the lake, visit the Crystal Palace. Perfect afternoon rest.
👆 Click for details

🍤 Where to Eat

🐷
Sobrino de Botín
Oldest restaurant in the world (1725). Hemingway's spot. Roast suckling pig. Book ahead.
👆 Click for details

🇭🇷 Rijeka — Croatia's Underrated Coastal Gem

Croatia's third-largest city, often overlooked for Dubrovnik and Split. Austro-Hungarian architecture, a lively café culture, and easy access to the stunning Kvarner Gulf islands. Gritty, authentic, cool.

🌟 Top Sights

🏰
Trsat Castle
Hilltop fortress with panoramic views over Rijeka and the Adriatic. 13th century. Free entry.
👆 Click for details
☕
Korzo Promenade
The city's main pedestrian street. Cafés, Habsburg buildings, city clock tower. Rijeka's living room.
👆 Click for details
🌊
Opatija (Day Trip)
20 min away. Belle Époque seaside resort. Lungomare coastal promenade, 12km of Adriatic views.
👆 Click for details

🇩🇪 Berlin — History, Art & Beer Gardens

A city shaped by its 20th-century scars and 21st-century creativity. The Wall is gone but everywhere remembered. World-class museums by day, legendary nightlife after dark, beer gardens in between.

🌟 Top Sights

Brandenburg Gate & Reichstag
Berlin's icon. Reichstag dome (free, book ahead) — glass spiral ramp with city views.
👆 Click for details
🎨
East Side Gallery
1.3km of the Berlin Wall covered in murals. Open-air, free, deeply moving.
👆 Click for details
🏛️
Museum Island
Five world-class museums on one island. Pergamon Altar, Nefertiti bust, Ishtar Gate.
👆 Click for details

🍺 Where to Eat & Drink

🍺
Prater Garten
Berlin's oldest beer garden (1837). 600 seats under chestnut trees. Pretzels, bratwurst, cold pilsner.
👆 Click for details

🇩🇪 Munich — Bavarian Charm & Alpine Views

Lederhosen aren't just for tourists — Bavarians actually wear them. Beer halls, Baroque palaces, and the Alps an hour away. The most livable big city in Germany.

🌟 Top Sights

🕰️
Marienplatz & Glockenspiel
The central square. The Rathaus-Glockenspiel performs daily at 11am & noon. Tourist trap? Yes. Worth it? Also yes.
👆 Click for details
🌊

Bigger than Central Park. Surfers on the Eisbach wave. Nude sunbathers (yes, really). Chinese Tower beer garden.
👆 Click for details

🍻 Where to Eat

🍺
Hofbräuhaus

🇨🇿 Prague — The City of a Hundred Spires

One of Europe's best-preserved medieval cities. Charles Bridge at dawn, Prague Castle looming above, cheap excellent beer. It's fairy-tale beautiful and surprisingly affordable.

Prague Castle & St. Vitus
Largest ancient castle in the world. St. Vitus Cathedral inside is a Gothic masterpiece.

🇸🇪 Stockholm — Beauty on Water

Built across 14 islands on Lake Mälaren and the Baltic Sea. Gamla Stan (Old Town) is one of Europe's best-preserved medieval centers. Clean, stylish, effortlessly cool.

🌟 Top Sights

Gamla Stan (Old Town)
Cobblestone streets, ochre buildings, the Royal Palace. Narrowest alley in Stockholm: Mårten Trotzigs Gränd.
👆 Click for details
🚢
Vasa Museum
A 17th-century warship that sank on its maiden voyage, perfectly preserved. The most visited museum in Scandinavia.
👆 Click for details

🇳🇴 Oslo — Fjords, Vikings & Modern Architecture

Surrounded by forest and fjord. The opera house you can walk on, Viking ships from the 9th century, and a sauna culture that ends with a jump in the freezing Oslofjord.

🌟 Top Sights

Oslo Opera House
Walk up the angled white marble roof. Panoramic views of the fjord and islands. Free, always open.
👆 Click for details
⚔️
Viking Ship Museum
Three actual Viking ships from the 9th century. Burial chambers, sleds, tools. Pure Norse history.
👆 Click for details

🇭🇺 Budapest — Thermal Baths & Ruin Bars

The Danube splits Buda (hilly, historic) from Pest (flat, vibrant). Thermal baths in grand 16th-century buildings, ruin bars in abandoned courtyards, and some of the best value dining in Europe.

🌟 Top Sights

Hungarian Parliament
Neo-Gothic masterpiece on the Danube. Book a guided tour inside — the crown jewels are there.
👆 Click for details
♨️
Széchenyi Thermal Bath
Largest medicinal bath in Europe. Outdoor pools, chess-playing old men, 38°C water. Pure Budapest.
👆 Click for details

🍽️ Where to Eat

🥞
Gundel
Budapest's most famous restaurant since 1894. Gundel palacsinta (flambéed crêpe). Splurge territory.
👆 Click for details

🇦🇹 Vienna — Imperial Grandeur & Coffee Houses

The former capital of the Habsburg Empire. Schönbrunn Palace, world-class opera, and UNESCO-listed coffee house culture. Elegant, clean, and surprisingly warm.

🌟 Top Sights

Schönbrunn Palace
The Habsburg summer palace. 1,441 rooms, gardens that rival Versailles. The Imperial Tour is essential.
👆 Click for details
☕
Café Central
The cathedral of Viennese coffee houses. Freud, Trotsky, and Lenin were regulars. Melange + Sachertorte.
👆 Click for details

🇳🇱 Amsterdam — Canals, Bikes & Golden Age Art

More canals than Venice, more bikes than people. Rijksmuseum for Rembrandt, Van Gogh Museum, canal boat rides, and brown cafés (bruin cafés) — cozy pubs unchanged for centuries.

🌟 Top Sights

🖼️
Rijksmuseum
Rembrandt's Night Watch, Vermeer, Dutch Masters. The grandest museum in the Netherlands.
👆 Click for details
📖
Anne Frank House
The hidden annex where Anne wrote her diary. Deeply moving. Book 6 weeks ahead.
👆 Click for details
🚤
Canal Cruise
See the canal ring (UNESCO World Heritage) from the water. Evening cruises with wine are the move.
👆 Click for details

🇵🇹 Lisbon — Seven Hills, Pastéis de Nata & Fado

Built on seven hills overlooking the Tagus River. Pastel-colored buildings, vintage trams, melancholic fado music, and the best-value dining in Western Europe. Bring good walking shoes — those hills are real.

🌟 Top Sights

🗼
Belém Tower & Jerónimos
UNESCO World Heritage. Manueline architecture. Get pastéis de nata at Pastéis de Belém — the original since 1837.
👆 Click for details
🎵
Alfama District
The oldest neighborhood. Narrow alleys, fado music drifting from taverns, miradouros (viewpoints).
👆 Click for details
🚋
Tram 28
The vintage yellow tram rattling through the city's prettiest streets. Go early morning to avoid crowds.
👆 Click for details

🍽️ Where to Eat

🦞
Cervejaria Ramiro
The temple of seafood. Garlic shrimp, percebes (goose barnacles), prego steak sandwich for dessert. Queue or book.
