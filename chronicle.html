🔧Core Features

Split-Pane THINK / SAYS

Left pane: the AI's step-by-step reasoning — every assumption, every source evaluation, every logical leap. Right pane: the polished conclusion. This is the difference between trusting the AI and verifying it. Security AI today is opaque. Chronicle is transparent by design.

Consensus: THINK/SAYS split

Immutable Investigation Timeline

Every prompt, every AI reasoning step, every user decision — permanently recorded in an append-only ledger. Becomes the canonical record of your investigation. Cannot be erased, even by rogue agents. Proves what was known and when it was known.

Consensus: append-only memory

Semantic Recall Across Investigations

Don't keyword-search. Ask: "Find similar phishing patterns from Q3 incidents" or "Show me all evidence related to the CFO's alibi". AI finds relevant records by meaning, not text match. Past investigations aren't dead files — they're a growing knowledge base.

Consensus: semantic retrieval

Human-in-the-Loop Gates

Any conclusion that becomes a permanent finding, any recommended action, any source evaluation — requires explicit human approval. Recorded in the timeline. The human is the investigator. The AI is the assistant. No black-box decisions.

Consensus: HITL approval gates

Multi-Session Persistence

Close an investigation and return days or weeks later. Full context restored — all prior reasoning, all sources, all findings. Essential for investigations that span months or SOC analysts working rotating shifts.

Consensus: multi-session persistence

Multi-Model Deliberation

Run a "threat brainstorming" or "hypothesis challenge" session where multiple AI models debate findings via OpenRouter. A DeepSeek model and a Claude model might flag different anomalies. Chronicle merges and curates their output.

Consensus + OpenRouter + Chimera

Row-Level Security

Multi-tenant ready. One Chronicle instance, multiple investigation teams. Each analyst sees only their cases. Granular permissions: some can view findings, others can approve conclusions. No data leaks between investigations.

Consensus: row-level security

🎯Concrete Use Cases

🛡️ SOC Analyst — Phishing Investigation

Analyst asks Chronicle to investigate a phishing email. AI reasons through headers, domain age, URL analysis, and similar past incidents. Full chain visible. Analyst approves the finding. Timeline records everything. Six months later, legal asks: "What did we know and when?" — the answer is in the ledger.

📰 Journalist — Source Verification

Reporter has conflicting claims from three sources. Chronicle cross-references timelines, flags contradictions, suggests follow-up questions. Every AI inference is traceable to a source. Editor reviews the reasoning chain before publication. No fact goes unverified.

⚖️ Law Firm — eDiscovery

Partner asks: "Find all documents where the defendant discussed pricing with competitors." Chronicle searches semantically — not just keyword hits. Reasoning chain shows why each document matched. Associate reviews findings, approves relevance tags. Audit trail is court-ready.

🔬 Researcher — Literature Review

Academic is synthesizing 200 papers on a topic. Chronicle reads each, extracts claims, flags contradictions. "This 2023 paper directly contradicts the 2021 consensus." Researcher sees the reasoning, approves connections, exports structured findings. Bibliography auto-generated from tracked sources.

🏢 Insurance — Claims Investigation

Adjuster investigating a disputed claim. Chronicle correlates timestamps, weather data, repair estimates, and claimant statements. Flags an inconsistency: "Claimant said roof damage occurred Tuesday, but weather data shows no storm that day." Adjuster approves finding. Case closed with evidence chain.

🕵️ OSINT Analyst — Person Investigation

Analyst researching a person of interest. Chronicle pulls public records, social media, news mentions. Cross-references aliases, locations, associates. Builds a timeline. Analyst sees every connection's provenance. Final report exports with full source attribution.

🎯Why Chronicle Wins

🔍 Transparent Reasoning

Splunk, CrowdStrike, Sentinel — none show the AI's reasoning. Chronicle's split-pane makes threat analysis verifiable rather than blindly accepted. Teams adopt tools they can audit. Trust is earned, not assumed.

📋 Court-Ready Evidence Chain

Every finding in Chronicle has a complete, immutable provenance trail. Who asked what question, what the AI reasoned, what the human approved. This isn't just good UX — it's legal defensibility for organizations that get sued over their investigations.

🧬 Pi Agent Pattern

Nobody needs to know Consensus exists. Chronicle is what people install. They love it, contribute plugins, find bugs, upstream fixes. The product drives the framework, not the other way around. This is how Pi Agent became popular — OpenClaw used it.

🔌 Investigation Marketplace

Community contributes "investigation packs": ransomware response playbook, phishing triage workflow, journalist source-verification template. Viral loop through shared playbooks. Every contribution strengthens both Chronicle and Consensus.

The insight: "I built a framework" gets zero users. "I built an investigation workbench that proves its conclusions" gets SOC teams, journalists, and law firms. Chronicle is the product. Consensus is the engine nobody sees — until they need to contribute to it.
